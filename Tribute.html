Dr. APJ Abdul Kalam

People's President and Missile Man of India

Biography Timeline Quotes

Biography

Early Life and Education:

Dr. Kalam was born into a modest Muslim family. His father, Jainulabdeen, was a boat owner, and his mother, Ashiamma, was a housewife. Despite financial constraints, Kalam was a bright student. He completed his school education at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School in Ramanathapuram. In 1954, he graduated with a degree in Physics from St. Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli.

Career in Aerospace Engineering:

In 1955, Dr. Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defense Research and Development Organization (DRDO) as a scientist. He played a crucial role in the development of India's first satellite launch vehicle (SLV) and the development of ballistic missile technology. Dr. Kalam also served as the project director for India's first indigenous satellite, "Aryabhata."

Role in Pokhran-II Nuclear Tests:

Dr. Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and the Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organization from 1992 to 1999. He played a pivotal role in India's Pokhran-II nuclear tests in 1998, earning him the nickname "Missile Man of India."

Presidency (2002-2007):

In 2002, Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam became the 11th President of India, succeeding K.R. Narayanan. During his presidency, Dr. Kalam focused on inspiring the youth, promoting education, and addressing societal issues.

Post-Presidential Activities:

After serving as President, Dr. Kalam continued to be actively involved in education and youth empowerment. He lectured at various institutions and interacted with students, emphasizing the importance of education and innovation.

Literary Contributions:

Dr. Kalam was an accomplished writer and authored several books, including "Wings of Fire," an autobiography, and "Ignited Minds," which emphasizes the role of youth in national development.

Death:

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam passed away on July 27, 2015, while delivering a lecture at the Indian Institute of Management Shillong. He suffered a cardiac arrest. Dr. Kalam's life is celebrated not only for his contributions to science and technology but also for his humility, vision, and dedication to the progress of India. His legacy continues to inspire countless individuals, especially the youth, to dream big and work towards the betterment of society.
